# Exercise — 2026-03-03b

## German → Italian
Translate the following sentences into Italian.

1. Ich habe Bücher auf dem Tisch.
   > Ho libri sul tavolo
   > ✓ Correct. "sul tavolo" perfect. Note: adding the partitive "dei" is more natural: "Ho dei libri sul tavolo" (I have some books).

2. Es gibt Erdbeeren im Kühlschrank.
   > Ci sono fragole nel frigorifero
   > ✓ Correct. "Ci sono" and "nel frigorifero" perfect. Note: "delle fragole" with partitive would be more complete.

3. Ich kaufe Schinken und Butter.
   > Compro prosciutto e burra
   > ✗ "burra" → "burro" — il burro (masculine!) = die Butter. Italian butter is masculine: il burro, not "la burra". Correct: "Compro del prosciutto e del burro."

4. Er hat Freunde in der Schule.
   > Lui ha amichi nella scuola.
   > ✗ "amichi" → "amici" — amico → amici (no h needed! the c before i is naturally soft, which is fine for this word). "a scuola" is more natural than "nella scuola" but both work. Partitive "degli amici" would be ideal.

5. Die Kirche ist weiß.
   > La chiesa e bianca.
   > ✗ "e" → "è" — missing accent! è = is (sein), e = and (und). This was mastered — don't let it slip back!

6. Ich habe zwölf Eier gekauft.
   > Ho comprato dodici uova.
   > ✓ Perfect! "comprato" spelled correctly (was "comoprato" last exercise), "dodici uova" correct. Great improvement!

7. Die Erdbeeren sind rot.
   > Le fragole sono rosse
   > ✓ Perfect! Color + feminine plural agreement correct.

8. Der Kopf ist groß.
   > La testa è grande
   > ✓ Perfect! Correctly used feminine "la testa" even though der Kopf is masculine in German. Body part vocabulary working.

9. Außerdem spreche ich auch Deutsch.
   > Nonostante anche parlo tedesco.
   > ✗ "Nonostante" → "Inoltre" — nonostante = obwohl/trotz (despite), außerdem = inoltre (moreover/in addition). Same connector confusion as before! Correct: "Inoltre parlo anche tedesco."

10. Er lernt, ich hingegen arbeite.
    > Studia, io invece lavoro.
    > ✓ Perfect! "invece" used correctly for "hingegen". Verb forms correct.

11. Wir haben fünfzehn Kekse.
    > Abbiamo quindici biscotti.
    > ✓ Perfect! Number and noun both correct.

12. Der Fuß ist klein.
    > Il piede è piccolo.
    > ✓ Perfect! Body part + adjective correct.

13. Es gibt Tomaten und Paprika im Kühlschrank.
    > Ci sono pomodori e paprika nel frigorifero
    > ✗ "paprika" → "peperoni" — die Paprika = i peperoni in Italian. "Paprika" exists in Italian only as the spice powder, not the vegetable. Correct: "Ci sono dei pomodori e dei peperoni nel frigorifero."

14. Die Nase ist lang und die Augen sind grün.
    > Il naso e lungo e i occhi sono verde
    > ✗ Three errors: (1) "e lungo" → "è lungo" — accent missing again! (2) "i occhi" → "gli occhi" — before vowels, masculine plural article is "gli" not "i". (3) "verde" → "verdi" — adjective must agree in number (plural → verdi).

15. Das heißt, wir essen um acht Uhr.
    > Questio significa (hab ich geoogelt) mangiamo alle otto.
    > ✗ "Questo significa" → "Cioè" — "das heißt" as a discourse connector = "cioè" (one word, very common). "Questo significa" is a literal translation that sounds stiff. Also "Questio" → "Questo" (spelling). Good effort looking it up though, and "alle otto" is correct!

16. Es gibt Studenten in der Bibliothek.
    > Ci sono studenti nella biblioteca
    > ✓ Correct. "nella biblioteca" good. Note: "degli studenti" with partitive would be more complete (degli before s+consonant).

17. Ich wasche mir die Hände, weil sie schmutzig sind.
    > Mi lavo le mani perchè sono sporce.
    > ✗ Two errors: (1) "sporce" → "sporche" — same sche-rule as fresche! sporco → sporche (c + h + e = hard c sound). (2) "perchè" → "perché" (acute accent é, not grave è). The sentence structure with perché is correct though!

18. Jedenfalls gehen wir in den Park.
    > Comunque (hab ich gegoogelt) adiamo in parco.
    > ✗ "comunque" is correct — good lookup! But: "adiamo" → "andiamo" (missing n); "in parco" → "al parco" (andare uses a + il = al parco, not "in parco"). Correct: "Comunque, andiamo al parco."

19. Das Herz ist rot.
    > Il cuore è rosso.
    > ✓ Perfect! Body part + color correct.

20. Ich habe sieben Orangen und vier Karotten gekauft.
    > Ho comprato sette arancie e quattro carote.
    > ✗ "arancie" → "arance" — the -cia/-gia plural rule: arancia → arance (drop the i because -cia is preceded by a consonant: n). Compare: ciliegia → ciliegie (vowel before → keep i). "quattro carote" is correct!

## Summary
10/20 correct. Body parts are solid (testa ✓, piede ✓, cuore ✓, naso ✓), colors are working (rosse ✓, rosso ✓, grande ✓), numbers are correct (dodici ✓, quindici ✓, sette/quattro ✓), and "invece" is great. The è accent slipped back twice (#5, #14) — watch for this, it was mastered! The sche-rule from fresche didn't transfer to "sporche" — same pattern, different word. Discourse connectors remain tricky: nonostante ≠ inoltre (#9), and cioè would be much better than "questo significa" (#15). Good instinct googling "comunque" and "questo significa" — just need to learn the short Italian connectors (cioè, inoltre, comunque, invece) as vocabulary.
