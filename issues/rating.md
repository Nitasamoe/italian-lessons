## Study Recommendations
- **TOP PRIORITY: è vs e.** Write out 10 sentences with "è" (is) and 10 with "e" (and). The accent changes the meaning completely. Try this mnemonic: "è" = "equals" (both start with e and mean "is").
- **Verb conjugation tables:** Write out the full loro forms for -ire verbs: dormono, aprono, capiscono, partono, sentono. The ending is always **-ono**, never -anno (that's -are verbs!).
- **Present tense vs past participle:** "aprire" → present: apro/apri/apre/apriamo/aprite/aprono. Past participle: aperto. Don't mix them.
- **Singular vs plural:** Match the number to what you're translating. "Die Studenten" (plural) = "gli studenti" (not "studente").
- Continue practicing passato prossimo — it's coming along well.
